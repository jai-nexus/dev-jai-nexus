# Challenge - motion-0036

## Main concern
A Claude integration layer can easily become either too generic to help or too provider-specific to age well. If the pack is not grounded in the existing substrate, it risks becoming just another prompt document rather than a durable governed interface.

## Risks
1. **Generic setup risk**
   - The artifacts may read like generic AI instructions instead of real dev-jai-nexus guidance.

2. **Prompt-bloat risk**
   - The Claude pack may become too large or too noisy for practical project use.

3. **Substrate disconnect risk**
   - The pack may fail to reflect the constitution, repo capsule schema, motion packet schema, slot policy, and scoring rubric already established in motion-0035.

4. **Workflow ambiguity**
   - The repo may gain a `CLAUDE.md` file without a clear explanation of how it should interact with motion governance and context bundles.

5. **Premature expansion risk**
   - The motion may drift into broader provider orchestration or multi-repo synchronization before the local Claude setup is made solid.

## Required safeguards
- keep the pack compact and immediately usable,
- derive the Claude-facing pack from governed substrate artifacts,
- preserve repo-specific terminology,
- keep the repo-root CLAUDE.md practical and implementation-oriented,
- define a clear minimal bootstrap set rather than "upload everything."

## Challenger standard for approval
I approve this motion only if:
- the Claude-facing artifacts are clearly useful for immediate setup,
- the pack is grounded in motion-0035 substrate artifacts,
- the repo-root CLAUDE.md is practical and repo-specific,
- the bootstrap set is small enough to reuse consistently,
- the motion remains scoped to local Claude integration.
