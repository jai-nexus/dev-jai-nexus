# Challenge - motion-0044

## Main concern
A validation-preset motion can become either too vague to help or too rigid to be practical. If the presets do not reflect real session patterns from motions 0040 through 0043, they will become another low-signal documentation layer.

## Risks
1. **Too-generic risk**
   - The presets may be so broad that they do not actually help future sessions.

2. **Over-rigidity risk**
   - The presets may be treated as mandatory law even when the task clearly needs different validation.

3. **Session drift risk**
   - The profiles may fail to reflect the actual recurring patterns observed in recent Claude live-use proofs.

4. **Documentation bloat risk**
   - The preset layer may become longer and noisier than the friction it is meant to remove.

5. **Authority confusion**
   - The preset layer may be mistaken for canonical repo truth instead of reusable session guidance.

## Required safeguards
- keep the preset set small,
- tie the profiles to real recurring task types,
- state clearly that presets guide but do not replace judgment,
- keep the artifacts compact and directly reusable,
- preserve the distinction between guidance and source truth.

## Challenger standard for approval
I approve this motion only if:
- the presets are clearly useful for future Claude sessions,
- the profiles reflect real session patterns already observed,
- the artifacts are compact enough to reuse,
- the guidance preserves operator judgment,
- the motion stays tightly scoped.
