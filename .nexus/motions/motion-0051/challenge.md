# Challenge (motion-0051)

## Risks
- Only one score distribution was tested. Edge cases such as tied totals with
  different breakdowns, boundary scores (0 and 10), or near-equal totals were
  not exercised.
- Only the Librarian panel was runtime-tested. Other panels share the same
  scoring core but were not individually exercised with differentiated scores.
- The test used manually populated scores, not scores generated by an actual
  selector or agent. This proves the math and ranking, not the end-to-end
  scoring workflow.

## Objections
- One clear differentiated distribution is sufficient to prove weighted total
  computation and winner determination. The scoring formula is deterministic:
  additional distributions would exercise the same arithmetic path.
- Edge-case testing (ties, boundaries) is a valid follow-up but not required
  for this proof target, which is differentiated-score ranking behavior.
- All panels share panelSelectCore.mjs. Testing one panel with differentiated
  scores is a reasonable basis for inference about shared behavior, calibrated
  as such.

## Mitigations
- Pre-calculated expectations were compared against actual output and matched
  exactly.
- Three behavior paths were exercised: score recompute, ranking, winner
  determination.
- The proof is explicitly scoped to differentiated-score ranking, not
  exhaustive edge-case coverage.
