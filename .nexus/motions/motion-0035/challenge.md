# Challenge - motion-0035

## Main concern
A formal context substrate can easily become either too vague to be useful or too abstract to be operational. If the constitution and schemas are not grounded in real workflow needs, they may look impressive while failing to reduce drift in practice.

## Risks
1. **Over-abstraction risk**
   - The artifacts may become theoretical descriptions with little practical value for repo work, council motions, or context bundles.

2. **Schema bloat risk**
   - The repo capsule or motion packet schemas may become too large or too rigid for everyday use.

3. **Terminology drift risk**
   - The artifacts may rename or blur concepts already established in earlier motions, causing governance confusion.

4. **Slot-policy ambiguity**
   - The slot policy may describe providers or models directly instead of preserving role-first slot purpose and replaceable assignment logic.

5. **Scoring-rubric vagueness**
   - The rubric may be too generic to guide real selection between competing solutions.

6. **Premature centralization risk**
   - The motion may try to solve all future cross-repo constitutional synchronization rather than shipping a usable v0 in dev-jai-nexus.

## Required safeguards
- keep artifacts concrete and minimally sufficient,
- preserve existing JAI NEXUS terminology,
- define bounded structures, not endless prose,
- keep slot policy role-first rather than provider-first,
- keep the rubric usable for real patch and decision comparison,
- ship a local substrate first before attempting wider federation.

## Challenger standard for approval
I approve this motion only if:
- all five substrate artifacts exist,
- their responsibilities are clearly distinct,
- the artifacts align with actual dev-jai-nexus workflow,
- they can be used as a real context pack for future chats and councils,
- the motion remains scoped to v0 substrate formalization.
